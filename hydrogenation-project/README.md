## **Bench-Scale Hydrogenation System Design & Optimization**

### **Overview**
During my co-op at Pyran Inc., I designed, built, and operated bench-scale hydrogenation systems to support feedstock upgrading. The objective was to improve reaction performance and generate data to support integration into downstream production of 1,5-propanediol (1,5-PDO).

---

### **Problem**
Initial hydrogenation conditions did not fully maximize conversion and yield, and there was limited standardization in analytical methods for evaluating product quality. This created challenges in consistently assessing performance and scaling the process toward production.

---

### **Approach**

- Designed and assembled multiple bench-scale hydrogenation reactor systems  
- Conducted controlled experiments to evaluate the effects of:
  - Temperature  
  - Pressure  
  - Residence time  
- Analyzed experimental data to identify trends in conversion and yield  
- Developed and implemented internal analytical standards for consistent product testing  
- Iteratively refined operating conditions based on experimental results  

---

### **Results**

- Identified operating conditions that improved reaction conversion and overall process performance  
- Established standardized analytical methods to improve consistency in product evaluation  
- Generated reliable experimental data to support downstream process integration  

---

### **Impact**
This work improved the reliability and performance of the hydrogenation process at the bench scale and contributed to its readiness for integration into larger-scale production systems.
